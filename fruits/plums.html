How to store plums

Generally

Store plums on the counter at room temperature to allow them to ripen fully. Keep them out of direct sunlight and check daily for softness and a sweet aromaâ€”both signs they're ready to eat. Once ripe, transfer plums to the refrigerator to extend their shelf life. You can store them loose in the crisper drawer or in a paper bag to maintain airflow while keeping them cool. Refrigeration slows further ripening and helps preserve their flavor and texture for a few extra days.

Do not store with

Leafy greens, carrots and cucumbers. Learn more about what not to store together.
