How to store apples

Generally

Store apples on the counter at room temperature if you plan to use them within a few days. Keep them in a cool, dry spot away from direct sunlight to maintain their freshness. For long-term storage, place apples in the refrigerator, which helps slow down the ripening process and extends their shelf life. Alternatively, if you have a basement or a root cellar, store them there. That environment keeps apples fresh for weeks or even months, depending on the variety.

Do not store with

Bananas, leafy greens and carrots. Learn more about what not to store together.
